Reflection

The statistical and Data analysis and Visualisation units was enjoyable units. The units challenged you to not only perform statistical calculations, but also too actually look at the data and interpret your findings. The T-test is a great calculation to use when you want to compare the means of two groups, often used for hypothesis testing (Bevans, 2023). If you enjoy working with numbers and data, it is essential to learn and understand statistics, as the ability to apply statistical methods is crucial for analysing and interpreting information effectively. Regardless of the industry, statistics help individuals solve problems, it offers insight and support in any situation where relevant data can be collected, analysed and interpreted (MichiganTech, N.D).

Sometimes in courses, you learn a lot about a specific subject. Although a wide range of topics was covered in the statistics units, the exercises were short and to the point. Summary measures such as the mean, median, standard deviation, and interquartile range are tools that everyone can use in their daily lives. Most of us have used the mean before, we just know it as the average. Since the exercises were short, they allowed you to learn a little at a time, but what you did learn really stuck with you.

The visualization units helped with presenting and visualizing data; it helped with understanding when to use what graph. Most of us have used excel graphs before and then we use the 3D graphs because it looked visually appealing, instead, as we have learned graphs should be designed to be easily understandable and informative, not to be visually appealing.
