Case Study on Privacy

Ricardo works for the records department where he has access to files of property tax records. A researcher named Beth has access to numerical portion of the data but requires personal information such as names and addresses of certain parties to contact these parties for more information.

If Ricardo is not responsible for determining allowable access, should he release the names and addresses?
Since Ricardo is not responsible for determining allowable access, he should not release the names and addresses of parties as this data is regarded as personal identifiable information. Instead, he should consult the company’s data protection officer for approval.

Suppose Ricardo were responsible for determining allowable access to the files. What ethical issues would be involved in his deciding whether to grant access to Beth?
Before granting Beth access, Ricardo needs to ensure he has the consent of the individuals to share their information for research. He must also verify that Beth has a legitimate need for the data and will handle it responsibly. As stated by (Bos, 2020), Beth, as the researcher, must ensure than any information obtained from or shared by human subjects respects their dignity and autonomy and does not violate the interests of individuals.

Should Beth be allowed to contact the individuals involved? That is, should the Records department release individuals' names to a researcher? What are the ethical issues for the Records department to consider?
Beth should be allowed to contact individuals involved, because she needs to brief all individuals on the goal and purpose of the research (Bos, 2020), all participants retain the right to withdraw their cooperation at any time even after the study has been completed (Bos, 2020). Beth needs to ensure that all participants remain anonymous.

Suppose Beth contacts the individuals to ask their permission, and one-third of them respond giving permission, one-third respond denying permission, and one-third do not respond. Beth claims that at least one-half of the individuals are needed to make a valid study. What options are available to Beth?
All individuals need to give permission to Beth to use their information for research, if she only has permission from one-third, she will need to contact the non-respondents again to obtain permission or contact different individuals.The researcher must obtain informed consent from all participants for all research activities. This consent should specify the data being collected, how it will be stored, and with whom it may be shared. The researcher must then use the data strictly in accordance with the given consent (gov.uk, 2018).
