3. Replace categorical values with numerical values "One-hot Encoding"¶

In [17]:
# perform one hot encoding on feature "origin" to use in correlation heatmap

# columns to one-hot encode
cat_columns = ["origin"]

encoder = ColumnTransformer(
    transformers=[("onehot", OneHotEncoder(), cat_columns)], remainder="passthrough")

# transform
transformed = encoder.fit_transform(num_features)

# convert to dataframe
encoded_cols = encoder.named_transformers_["onehot"].get_feature_names_out(cat_columns)
all_columns = list(encoded_cols) + [col for col in num_features.columns if col not in cat_columns]
encoded_df = pd.DataFrame(transformed, columns=all_columns)

4. Correlation Heatmap¶

In [19]:
selected_columns = ['mpg', 'origin_1', 'origin_2', 'origin_3', 'cylinders', 'displacement', 'weight', 'acceleration']
corr_matrix = encoded_df[selected_columns].corr()
dataplot = sns.heatmap(corr_matrix.corr(), cmap="Purples", annot=True, annot_kws={"fontsize":5}, vmax=1)

mpg (miles per gallon) has a strong negative relationship to cylinders, displacement, weight and origin_1. The correlation between origin_1 and mpg has a strong negative relationship, suggesting that cars manufactured in this region tend to have a lower fuel efficiency compared to cars manufactured in origin_2 and 3

cylinders, displacement and weight are perfectly correlated to each other indicating multicollinearity among them.
