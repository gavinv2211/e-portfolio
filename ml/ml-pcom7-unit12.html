Final Reflection

The course provided a comprehensive understanding of the end-to-end process of building machine learning models.

In the first part of the course, I learned about exploratory data analysis (EDA), a crucial step in data science and machine learning. EDA focuses on understanding the data before making any assumptions, identifying obvious errors, detecting outliers, and uncovering patterns and relationships among features. EDA to me is like a science on its own. It is where we get a sense of the data, sparking innovative ideas for problem-solving or identifying opportunities, even without a specific problem to address.

As we progressed, we were introduced to statistical methods and supervised machine learning, including correlation and regression. Correlation is purely statistical but by understanding it helps machine learning by identifying relationships between independent variables and the dependent variable, which can guide feature selection and engineering. Regression is a statistical and machine learning technique used for predictive modelling where the goal is to predict continuous values.

We delved into artificial neural networks (ANNs) and convolutional neural networks (CNNs). Both are types of neural networks, but we learned about their distinct purposes, structures, and how they process data differently.

The final part of the course, focused on model selection and evaluation, which provided valuable insights into the approaches and techniques used to choose the most suitable models for specific tasks, as well as different methods used for evaluating and improving model performance.
